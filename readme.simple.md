# Evasion Attacks in Trading -- Explained Simply

Imagine a video game where you learn the pattern of a guard's patrol route, then sneak past by timing your moves perfectly. Evasion attacks work the same way -- they figure out a model's blind spots and exploit them to sneak bad data past its defenses.

## What Is an Evasion Attack?

Think of a trading computer as a very smart guard who watches numbers all day and decides when to buy or sell. This guard has learned patterns -- "when numbers look like THIS, I should buy."

An evasion attack is when a sneaky person figures out exactly what the guard is looking for and changes the numbers just a tiny bit -- so small you can barely notice -- but enough to trick the guard into making the wrong decision.

## How Does It Work?

Imagine you have a secret code to get into a clubhouse. The code is "knock three times." Now imagine someone watches you and figures out the code. They can now knock three times and get in too!

In trading, the "code" is the pattern the computer looks for. The attacker studies the pattern and then creates fake market data that looks normal but tricks the computer.

There are two ways to do this:

**The Easy Way (White Box)**: You can see inside the computer and know exactly what it is looking for. It is like having the answer key to a test -- you know exactly what to write.

**The Hard Way (Black Box)**: You cannot see inside the computer. You have to try different things and see what works, like guessing the password to someone's phone by trying different combinations.

## Why Is This Scary for Trading?

In a video game, if a guard gets tricked, you just move to the next level. But in trading, if the computer gets tricked, real money gets lost! And here is the really scary part: if you trick one computer, other computers watching the same numbers might get tricked too. It is like finding out that the same key opens many different locks.

## How Do We Defend Against It?

The best defense is to use MANY different guards instead of just one. If each guard watches for different things, it is much harder to sneak past all of them at once. It is like having a guard who watches the door, another who watches the windows, and another who watches the roof. Even if you fool one, the others will catch you!

## Key Ideas

1. **Evasion attacks** trick computers by changing data just a tiny bit
2. **White-box attacks** are easier because the attacker can see inside the computer
3. **Black-box attacks** are harder but still possible by trial and error
4. **Transferability** means a trick that works on one computer often works on others
5. **The best defense** is using many different computers that each look at things differently
